Trip to Spain

I recently had the opportunity to visit this beautiful country and I would like to share a little bit of my experience exploring this culture.

Spain:

"Spain's biggest airport is in Madrid, making it the most likely arrival point for most visitors to the country."
Madrid is the city where I stayed at and I completely fell in love with it !
From it's variation of delightful food to very friendly people.

  • Places I visited :

    • 1. Royal Palace of Madrid , is the official residence of the Spanish Royal Family , although now only used for state ceremonies.
    2. Plaza Mayor , is a major public space in the heart of Madrid.
    The Plaza Mayor , is for the people of Madrid and tourists to shop, walk around, eat, and enjoy the outdoors.
    3. Gran Vía , referred to as the "Spanish Broadway", is one of the city's most important shopping areas, with a large number of hotels.
    4. Outside Madrid , I visited the city of Toledo , located in central Spain.
    Is known as the "Imperial City" and as the "City of the Three Cultures" for the cultural influences of Christians, Muslims and Jews reflected in its history.

  • Transport:

    • "The Madrid Metro system is the 12th longest rapid transit system in the world"
    This was the best , fastest and efficient way to travel around Madrid.
    The price is reasonable and they also provide with a 10 pre-paid ticekts for $20 wich was really convenient.

  • Food:

    • Madrid has a variety of regional and international cuisines that is unparalleled anywhere else in Spain.
    I have to say that the food was delightful with great quality for the cheap price.

    The must-try typical food :

    1. Callos a la Madrileña ; is one of the typical foods during the winter.
    You’ll normal find this stew in a clay dish. It features strips of beef tripe (stomach), chunks of chorizo and slices of morcilla (blood sausage).
    2. Bocadillo de Calamares ; No trip to Madrid is complete without tasting the city’s most famous sandwich: the bocadillo de calamares, or fried squid sandwich.
    It consists of crusty, fresh bread loaded with flour-coated, deep-fried rings of squid, and nothing else.
    Some Spaniards top the two-ingredient creation with tomato and paprika puree or homemade garlic mayonnaise.
    3. Paella ; is almost always associated with seafood, chicken and vegetables.
    The dish was named after the pan it is cooked in, a Paellera, which is also used for a variety of rice dishes, such as arroz negro, as well as the Spanish pasta dish: fideua.
    4. Spanish Churros with Chocolate ,Churros are sweet, deep-fried dough treats.
    Usually they are dipped in a hot, thick, pudding-like chocolate, hence the name – Churros Con Chocolate.
    The popularity of these little guys is unbelievable.
